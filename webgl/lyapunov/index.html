Rendering Lyapunov Fractals on the GPU

From Wikipedia:

Markus–Lyapunov fractals are bifurcational fractals derived from an extension of the logistic map in which the degree of the growth of the population, $r$, periodically switches between two values $A$ and $B$.


The fractal is constructed by mapping the regions of stability and chaotic behaviour (measured using the Lyapunov exponent $\lambda$) in the $a \times b$ plane for given periodic sequences of $a$ and $b$. In the images below, color corresponds to $\lambda < 0$ (stability), and black corresponds to $\lambda>0$ (chaos). Color Interpolation is used given the degree of stability.

The exponent $\lambda$ is defined as follows: $$\lambda = \lim \limits_{N->\infty} \cdot \frac{1}{N} \sum \limits_{n=1}^{N} \log | \frac{dx_{n+1}}{dx_{n}} | \Rightarrow$$ $$\lambda = \lim \limits_{N->\infty} \cdot \frac{1}{N} \sum \limits_{n=1}^{N} \log | r_n ( 1 - 2x_n ) |$$ The ranges used for Lyapunov fractals are typically within the region $[0,4]\times[0,4]$ and the exponents are defined as above. Yet peculiar results are obtained by changing the $\log$ function as long as there is some sort of periodicity.
